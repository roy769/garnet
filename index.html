Garnet

Esoterically garnet is known as January's red birthstone however it's actually a silicate group with a variety of 6 mineral species.

(mindat.org)

Mineralogy:

  • Its hardness is between 6.5 and 8.5.
  • The crystal system is Isometric
  • A vitreous Luster
  • Cleavage is partial or none
  • (minerals.net)

Garnet Minerals
Mineral Composition Specific Gravity Hardness Colors
Almandine Fe3Al2(SiO4)3 4.20 7 - 7.5 red, brown
Pyrope Mg3Al2(SiO4)3 3.56 7 - 7.5 red to purple
Spessartine Mn3Al2(SiO4)3 4.18 6.5 - 7.5 orange to red to brown
Andradite Ca3Fe2(SiO4)3 3.90 6.5 - 7 green, yellow, black
Grossular Ca3Al2(SiO4)3 3.57 6.5 - 7.5 green, yellow, red, pink, clear
Uvarovite Ca3Cr2(SiO4)3 3.85 6.5 - 7 green
The compositions listed above are for end members of several solid solution series. There are a number of other garnet minerals that are less frequently encountered and not as important in industrial use. They include goldmanite, kimzeyite, morimotoite, schorlomite, hydrogrossular, hibschite, katoite, knorringite, majorite, and calderite.
(geology.com)

By comparing the table to gem chart we see how color transitions with the different combinations of the Alkaline Earth Metals Ca, Mg, with Transitional Metals Al, Fe, Cr.

Garnet can be found in all varieties of rocks. Although mostly found in metasedimentary rock with a high aluminum content such as shale is subjected regional metamorphism and then produces schist. Contact metamorphism is another way garnet is formed at subsurface magma chambers, lava flows, deep-source volcanic eruptions.

(geology.com)

    Utillization

  • Abrasives

    • Due to their hardness and crystal system, when pulverized are great tools for abrasive balsting. This process is much faster than sanding when water is added.

  • Water cutting

    • Its major industrial use is to cut metal in the mining industry. Utilization produces very little dust.

  • Filtration

    • Garnets porous space allows fluid to pass but keep contanimants at bay.

(geology.com)

Mining

Some garnets are brought up with volcanic eruptions. These larger pieces are known as xenoliths. These xenoliths are indicators for diamond discovery.

Mining is not restricted to one area garnet is gathered on a world wide level.

(geology.com)
